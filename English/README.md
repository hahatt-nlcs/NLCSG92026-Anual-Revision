# Grade 9 English Language & Literature Assessment  
## *Purple Hibiscus* – Exam Guide

**Date:** Wednesday, 15th April  

---

## Exam Overview

- One **extended analytical response**
- Focus: *Adichie’s presentation of a theme* in *Purple Hibiscus*
- **Closed-book exam** (no extract provided)
- You must show knowledge of the **entire novel**

---

## Key Expectations

- Use:
  - Short quotations **OR**
  - References to key moments
- Focus on:
  - **What** Adichie suggests about the theme  
  - **How** she presents it  
  - **Why** she presents it this way  

---

## Essay Structure

Your response should include:

1. **Introduction**
2. **Body Paragraphs**
3. **Conclusion**

- Refer to both:
  - Specific moments  
  - The novel as a whole  

---

## Possible Themes

- Silence  
- Violence  
- Religion  
- Family  

---

## Time Management (80 Minutes Total)

- **5 min** → Read & unpack the question  
- **10 min** → Plan your response  
- **60 min** → Write your essay  
- **5 min** → Proofread & edit  

---

## Assessment Criteria

### 1. Analysis

- Identify and analyse key aspects of the text  
- Explore the writer’s choices  
- Justify ideas with examples and terminology  
- Compare features across different parts of the text  

---

### 2. Organisation

- Respond clearly to the prompt  
- Present ideas logically  
- Use:
  - Paragraphing  
  - Topic sentences  
  - Signposting  
  - Discourse markers  

---

### 3. Language Use

- Use varied vocabulary and sentence structures  
- Maintain appropriate academic tone  
- Ensure:
  - Correct grammar  
  - Accurate punctuation  
  - Accurate spelling  

---

## Revision Strategies

### Content Review
- Reread the novel / revisit key scenes  
- Review:
  - Booklets  
  - LitCharts (Firefly)  
  - Annotations / Cornell notes  
  - Class notes and theme trackers  

---

### Active Revision

- Create **flashcards** (quotes + themes)  
- Build **essay plans** for key themes:
  - Silence vs voice  
  - Power and control  
  - Freedom  
  - Truth and consequences  

---

### Skills Practice

- Analyse key **symbols** and their meanings  
- Practise linking symbols to themes  
- Revise key quotations and apply:
  - Literary terminology (e.g. *pathetic fallacy*, motif, symbolism)  

---

## Final Tip

Focus less on memorising long quotes and more on:
- Clear argument  
- Strong analysis  
- Confident use of evidence
